Mango Marmalades

In this jam you will find 2 ways to make mango jam (with sugar, no sugar), because their sugars are high

Ingredients

1 - 3 cups ripe mango

2 -2 cups sugar

3 - water

4 - ascorbic acid

Procedure

1 - Cut the handle into thirds. Remove the pulp with a fork or peel it beforehand..

2 - Cook the mango with the sugar in a saucepan for 25 to 40 minutes or until thickened. The color should be darker than the original yellow, but not darker than a medium orange..

3 - Crush the handle. Using a fork or large spoon, mash until it becomes jam-like.

4 - Dissolve the ascorbic acid in hot water, and add it to the mango-sugar mixture..

5 - Process it and put it in preserves

Recipe Note

For every 230 to 290 g or 1 small jar of mango jam, you will need to add about 4 to 6 times more than 500 mg of ascorbic acid, as you will not be using any artificial preservatives. If the average sugar is 1.1 (1 cup mango per 1 cup sugar), then you can keep it for 5 to 6 months without refrigeration. Those who are more health-conscious may prefer to use a lower ratio, but their shelf life will also be shorter.

Low Sugar Mango Marmalade

1 - 3 medium sized handles

2 - 1 ¼ cups of water

3 - 1/4 cup sugar

4 -2 tablespoons lemon juice

5 - 1 teaspoon lemon zest

6 - pinch powdered ginger

Procedure

1 - Peel the mangoes. Cut them into cubes.

2 - Boil the water with the brown sugar, lemon juice, lemon zest and powdered ginger.

3 - Add mango cubes when boiling mixture begins to thicken.

4 - Crush the mango cubes while they're cooking. When it starts to boil, bring it to a simmer.

5 - Continue to cook until it acquires the consistency of marmalade..

6 - Preserve the jam. Do it with an appropriate canning technique to ensure safety (keeping it refrigerated).
